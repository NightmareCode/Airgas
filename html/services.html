Gas Types & Applications

Breathing Air BA

Clean, filtered compressed air for breathing systems and industrial use.

Industries

  • Healthcare and hospitals
  • Metal fabrication and welding
  • Water treatment facilities
  • Chemical manufacturing
  • Aerospace

Why It's Important

Breathing air provides a safe air supply for SCBA and airline systems in hazardous environments. Properly conditioned air also powers pneumatic tools and supports maintenance work and confined space operations.

Nitrogen N₂

Inert gas used for purging, blanketing, and creating oxygen-free environments.

Industries

  • Food and beverage
  • Electronics manufacturing
  • Pharmaceutical production
  • Oil and gas
  • Chemical processing

Why It's Important

Nitrogen prevents spoilage and contamination by displacing oxygen. In food packaging, it extends shelf life. In electronics, it prevents oxidation during manufacturing.

N₂

Argon Ar

Noble gas primarily used as a shielding gas in welding and inert atmospheres.

Industries

  • Metal fabrication (TIG/MIG)
  • Specialty metals (titanium)
  • Electronics production
  • Light bulb manufacturing
  • Scientific research

Why It's Important

Argon's complete inertness protects welds from atmospheric contamination, resulting in stronger, cleaner welds. For specialty metals like titanium, argon shielding is absolutely necessary.

Ar

Carbon Dioxide CO₂

Used for carbonation, refrigeration, pH control, and welding shielding.

Industries

  • Food and beverage
  • Welding (MIG shielding)
  • Water treatment
  • Fire suppression systems
  • Greenhouses

Why It's Important

CO₂ gives beverages their fizz and is essential for food preservation as dry ice. In welding, it's a cost-effective shielding gas. Its ability to regulate pH makes it valuable in water treatment.
